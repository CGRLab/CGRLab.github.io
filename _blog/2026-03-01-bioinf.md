You want to do research in bioinformatics and you studied computer science, engineering or quantitive filed with no biology background ? Bioinformatics is a huge filed there are several master or undergrad program and courses. But we want to jump into research,  it was overwhelmimng for me in the beigning, as a person without  biology background I started learing the needed background for each project. For quick start on the DNA sequence line of research I recommadn these:



1- Genetics-101 [Link](https://health.hawaii.gov/genetics/files/2013/05/Completed-Genetics-101-for-Teacher-Final.pdf).
2- DNA sequencing technologies: ACM Computing Surveys [Link](https://dl.acm.org/doi/abs/10.1145/3340286).
Molecular Biology for Computer Scientists by Hunter [Link](https://tandy.cs.illinois.edu/Hunter_MolecularBiology.pdf).
3- Bioinformatics: An Introduction for Computer Scientists. [Link](https://doi.org/10.1145/1031120.1031122).

It would be also useful to learn from online courses: 

1- Eric Landres' MIT course "Introduction to Biology - The Secret of Life" on Edx [Link](https://mitxonline.mit.edu/courses/course-v1:MITxT+7.00x/)

2- Bioinformatics Specialization on coursera by Pavel Pevzner  [Link](https://www.coursera.org/specializations/bioinformatics).
 
3- Genomic Data Science Specialization on Coursera by Steven Salzberg, Ben Langmead, and others. [Link](https://www.coursera.org/specializations/genomic-data-science).
