1. Familirizeing yourself with concepts

Here is a list of review papers to start with:
 - Deep learning: new computational modelling techniques for genomics. Nature Reviews Genetics, 2019, [Paper](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41576-019-0122-6).
- Transformers and genome language models. Nature Machine Intelligence, 2025 [Paper]( https://www.nature.com/articles/s42256-025-01007-9).
- Genomic language models: opportunities and challenges. Trends in Genetics, 2025. [Paper](https://www.cell.com/trends/genetics/fulltext/S0168-9525(24)00295-6).


And these are some introductory YouTube videos: 
-  Large Language Models in Computational Biology by Jian Ma [Link](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aBWJomCzkok) 43mins.

- MIA Primer: Gokcen Eraslan, A Primer on DNA Foundation Modeling [Link](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3QcRyuhHUn4) 61mins.



2. Familirizeing yourself with the literature

- Species-aware DNA language models capture regulatory elements and their evolution. Genome Biology, 2024, [Paper](https://doi.org/10.1186/s13059-024-03221-x).
- A DNA language model based on multispecies alignment predicts the effects of genome-wide variants. Nature Biotechnology, 2025, [Paper](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41587-024-02511-w).
- Predicting functional constraints across evolutionary timescales with phylogeny-informed genomic language models [Paper](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.09.21.677619).

Next steps include finding the knowledge gap and room for improvement. It is also important to tun tools on small datasets and see their outpouts.
